Proprietary Process

Skin Coat Technology

A precision co-extrusion process that permanently fuses a colored outer skin onto the wire in a single pass — enabling custom color, lower minimums, and better pricing without sacrificing performance.

How It Works

Three extruders. One pass. One fused product.

Unlike conventional single-extruder wire production, Skin Coat uses a main extruder and two co-extruders operating simultaneously.

Step 01

Main Extruder

Applies the inner layer of natural (un-pigmented) compound over the copper conductor — forming the core insulation.

Step 02

Co-Extruders Apply Skin

Two smaller co-extruders alternately apply the colored outer skin — 0.004" to 0.010" thick depending on wire gauge and type.

Step 03

Fused in the Melt

Both layers are applied simultaneously while material is in a molten state under pressure — fusing into one distinct, inseparable form.

What makes it different

  • Color won't peel, scrape off, or separate. The skin is fused at a molecular level in the melt — not painted or coated afterward.
  • Identical electrical and physical properties to conventionally produced PVC insulated wire — no trade-offs.
  • Reduced scrap rates and machine downtime compared to conventional color extrusion — savings passed to the customer.
  • Lower minimum order quantities — custom colors are practical for smaller runs that would be uneconomical conventionally.
