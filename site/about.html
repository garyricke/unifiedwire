Chapter 1 · 1965 · Chicago, Illinois

The harness company

Twelve years before Unified Wire & Cable existed, our founder was already in the wire business — just from the customer side. He ran a wiring harness manufacturing operation in Chicago, building assemblies for industrial and commercial OEMs.

Like every harness shop, he bought spools of finished wire from outside suppliers. And like every harness shop, he watched lead times slip, quality drift between lots, and pricing be dictated by middlemen who had no stake in his output.

"Why not get more integrated?"

That question was the seed. Rather than absorb the volatility, he decided to build the wire company that would supply his harness business — and others. The plan: own the copper, own the draw, own the extrusion. Control the variables you can.

Chapter 2 · 1977 · Founded

Unified Wire & Cable is born

In 1977, vertical integration was uncommon in the wire industry. Most manufacturers bought rod, drew it, extruded it — and outsourced everything else. The new company took the opposite stance.

The name was deliberate. "Unified" wasn't a brand flourish; it was a description. Copper fabrication, wire drawing, stranding, and extrusion would all happen at one address, under one quality system, accountable to one team.

One name. One supply chain. One quality standard.

The decisions made in those early years still shape who picks up the phone today. Tim Foley — the founder's son — joined the sales organization 25 years ago and now leads it. Jorge Hernandez, the second person ever hired, still runs the production floor more than three decades later.

This is not a company that turns over. It is a company that compounds.

Chapter 3 · 1983 · DeKalb, Illinois

Ninety miles west of Chicago

By the early 1980s, the harness business needs more room than its three-story Wrigleyville walk-up can provide. Both Electromechanical Devices (the harness side) and Unified Wire & Cable relocate to DeKalb, Illinois — ninety miles west of the city, off I-88, on a single-floor industrial parcel with room to grow.

Unified, still small, leases space inside its sister company. One extruder. One person running it: Brian Foley, the founder's eldest son, by himself. The harness business is the engine; the wire business is its quiet, vertically integrated apprentice.

"It was just my brother. That was all."

That arrangement holds for almost a decade. The team is small, the product range narrow, but the foundations of what would become a vertically integrated wire plant are quietly being laid — one production run at a time.

Chapter 4 · 1992 · A building of our own

Twenty thousand square feet, three or four people

Year-over-year demand starts to outgrow the leased corner inside the harness company. In 1992, Unified breaks ground on its own building next door — a 20,000 sq ft facility built specifically for wire, with one extruder and a team of three or four.

"We had growth, year after year. So we needed more space."

The building itself is the bet. Room for a second extruder, then a third. Room for stranding, then bunching. Room to bring copper draw in-house. Over the next two decades, that 20,000 sq ft expands incrementally to 65,000 — until the lot itself runs out of land.

By 2011, an adjacent building becomes available. Unified buys it, ties the two together, and the horseshoe-shaped footprint that defines the operation today is set.

Chapter 5 · 2024 · A new chapter

Joining Mueller Industries

Heading toward fifty years, Unified Wire & Cable becomes part of Mueller Industries — a publicly traded multi-billion-dollar parent and one of the largest copper and copper-alloy manufacturers in North America.

The acquisition unlocks a scale of investment that would take a family business decades to fund organically. The thesis is straightforward: the team in DeKalb knows how to make wire, has the customer relationships to grow it, and now has the capital to extend into new product categories.

"We're not afraid to invest where it makes sense."

Medium voltage is the first major bet — a brand-new product line at 15kV to 69kV, built on top of the low-voltage foundation Unified has refined since 1977. More to follow.

Chapter 6 · Today · DeKalb, Illinois

Approaching fifty years

Nearly half a century after that first decision in Chicago, the philosophy is intact — and the operation has scaled around it. Unified Wire & Cable manufactures from a 100,000 square foot horseshoe-shaped facility in DeKalb, Illinois, 1.5 hours west of Chicago.

The Midwest address is a logistics asset: shorter transit to OEMs and distributors across the continental U.S. than coastal alternatives. Seven extrusion lines run 24/7, spanning the full breadth of the catalog — from automotive hook-up wire to MIL spec constructions — and now extending into 15kV to 69kV medium voltage, a brand-new product category in 2026.

What hasn't changed is the team. Average tenure on the production floor exceeds a decade. Defective parts per million sit around 300. On-time delivery clears 95%. The institutional knowledge that comes with that kind of continuity is the asset that no spec sheet captures — and the reason customers keep calling back.
