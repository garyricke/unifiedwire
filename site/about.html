Our Story

The name says it all.

In 1965, our founder was running a wiring harness manufacturing company in Chicago — buying wire on the outside like everyone else. Watching his supply chain introduce delays, quality variability, and pricing he couldn't control, he made a decision: vertically integrate.

In 1977, Unified Wire & Cable was founded — named for that philosophy of integration. By owning copper fabrication, extrusion, and production under one roof, we could guarantee what no outside supplier could: consistency from rod to finished spool.

His son, Tim Foley, has led the sales organization for 25 years. Jorge Hernandez — the second person ever hired — still manages the production floor. This is not a company that turns over. It is a company that compounds.

Vertical Integration

From copper rod to finished wire.
All under one roof.

Most wire manufacturers buy pre-fabricated inputs. We start earlier. That difference in control translates directly into advantages for our customers.

Copper Fabrication

In-house copper rod fabrication — raw material control from the start.

Wire Drawing

Copper drawn to precise gauge — tightly controlled diameter and stranding.

Extrusion

7 extrusion lines applying PVC, XLPE, nylon, and specialty compounds.

QC & Shipment

Quality inspection and finished goods — reels and drums ready for immediate shipment.

Raw Material Security

In-house copper fabrication protects customers from third-party supply disruptions. When the market tightens, we don't scramble — we draw.

Custom Agility

Owning the full process enables faster prototyping of non-standard constructions — unusual strand counts, custom insulation thickness, specialty compounds.

Cost Control

Vertical integration buffers against some raw material volatility — and eliminates the margin layers that accumulate when suppliers pass material between hands.
