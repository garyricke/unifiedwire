Executive summary

Everything is ready except the data.

In the May kick-off we scoped the Spec Navigator as a 12-week build so customers could search, filter, and compare Unified products and pull the right spec sheet in seconds. That tool now exists as a working engine — it just runs on placeholder products.

The single unblocker

To go from prototype to a real, launch-ready tool, we need one structured list of your products — the families you make, the sizes and colors in each, and their key specs. That's it. This document breaks down precisely what we need, shows how a couple hundred cells (not 20,000 rows) covers your whole catalog, and proposes a way for your team to keep it current without learning any new software.

01 · What we're building

A tool that makes finding a wire spec take 60 seconds, not 10 minutes.

Southwire and Hubbell make customers do homework. The Spec Navigator is active — it guides an engineer or buyer straight to the right product and its spec sheet, three ways in.

Three ways in

By application ("I'm wiring a data center"), by spec (600V, 12 AWG, THHN, copper), or by fuzzy search that forgives typos and jargon.

Instant filtering

No page reloads. Results narrow live as gauge, voltage, temperature and standards are selected — faster than clicking through categories.

Compare & cross-ref

Line up to four wires side by side, and type a competitor's part number to surface the Unified equivalent — steals share directly.

Live spec sheets

Every result links straight to its spec sheet — the same print suite we've already built — and can generate a branded PDF on the fly.

04 · Why this is far less work than it sounds

You describe families. We generate the 20,000 SKUs.

A full wire catalog is 15,000–25,000 SKUs once you multiply gauge × color × put-up. Nobody should ever hand-type that. Instead, you give us the recipe at the family level and the tool expands it automatically.

You provide
THHN/THWN-2
Copper
one family block
+a size table — ampacity, O.D., weight per gauge (14 AWG → 1000 MCM)
+a color list — black, red, blue, green, white…
+your put-ups — 500 ft coil, 2,500 ft reel…
→ we auto-expand into every valid SKU, each with its own page & spec sheet
What this means for your workload

Across your whole line, that's roughly 50–100 family blocks and a size table each — on the order of a few hundred cells your team actually maintains, versus tens of thousands of rows. This is also the direct answer to the March/April "categories vs. SKUs?" question: both — every product, organized by family, with the size and color variations generated for you.

We'll send a pre-built sheet with the exact columns, drop-downs for the fixed fields (voltage, temperature, standards, markets), and the THHN example above already filled in — so your team just follows the pattern. Tell us which you prefer and we'll set it up.

06 · Keeping it current, forever

One place to edit. No new app to learn.

The goal is that keeping the tool current is easier than any software you'd expect to use. Here are three ways to do it — our recommendation first. This is a proposal; tell us what fits your team.

Recommended

A shared master sheet

One Google Sheet is the single source of truth; the live tool syncs from it.

  • It isn't an app — your team already knows spreadsheets
  • Built-in revision history & comments
  • Edit a cell, the site reflects it on sync
  • Multiple people, one canonical place

An on-site admin form

A password-gated add/edit screen built into the website itself.

  • Edit directly on the site
  • More to build & maintain
  • Feels more like "an app" to log into

An Excel template you email

You edit a spreadsheet and send it over; we re-import each round.

  • Familiar and offline-friendly
  • No live sync — changes wait on a round-trip
  • Version control lives in your inbox
